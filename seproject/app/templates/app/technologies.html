COVID Related Technologies Dealing with Health and Operations

At Columbus State University, there will be no telehealth available because there is no platform in place for it, and there are not enough staff available to solely focus on procedures involving telehealth. If a student has symptoms for COVID-19, the staff at the Health Center will propose being able to do rapid COVID-19 testing, rapid flu A/B and rapid strep testing.

Administrators have made it so where all client forms will be digitalized and made available for students to fill out in the computer lab. If there were to be another semester at Columbus State University that would need to become 100% online, then programs and activities would be evaluated on an individual basis to determine whether there are other options for them or if they need to be postponed.

Columbus State University would need to partner with GDPH, and/or Piedmont or St. Francis in order to administer not all, but some of the tests. Anyone who steps on campus will have access to the walk-through scanners and hand-held thermometers that have been purposefully placed all over campus.
